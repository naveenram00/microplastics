Everyday Life

In a modernized, consumerist society, plastics are a foundational aspect of our everyday lives. From car tires, laptops, phones, and TVs to packaging, furniture, building, and food and water, plastics now do it all. We rely on them for nearly every aspect of the economy and thus have developed a dependence upon their use. Paired with high population abundance near watersheds, this dependency creates a high concentration of microplastics flowing into waterways each and every day. Actions as simple as driving create microplastic pollution, as tire abrasions find their way into tributaries or storm drains and eventually lakes or the ocean.
